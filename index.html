What is the LOTUS TOWER

Lotus Tower, also referred to as Colombo Lotus Tower, is a tower of 356 m (1,168 ft), located in Colombo, Sri Lanka. It also reflects the symbolic landmark of Sri Lanka. As of 16 September 2019, the tower is currently the tallest self-supported structure in South Asia. It is also the second tallest structure in South Asia after the guy-wire-supported INS Kattabomman in India. The tower is also the 11th tallest completed tower in Asia and it is also the 19th tallest tower in the world. It was first proposed to be built in the suburb of Peliyagoda but later the Government of Sri Lanka decided to shift the location.The lotus-shaped tower will be used for communication, observation and other leisure facilities, with construction costing $104.3 million, funded by EXIM Bank of People’s Republic of China. It is visible throughout Colombo, its suburbs and most major highways radiating from and around the city.

  • The tallest multi-purpose tower in Sri Lanka, the Lotus Tower, is scheduled to be re-opened in September 2022, sans broadcasting facilities. In 2011, the then government of the day promoted, and proposed a multi-purpose tower which was expected to provide facilities for broadcasting, telecommunications/defence activities. Its dual purpose was also to showcase the local attractions from the tower top with multi-faceted public facilities sprinkled on the ground. Indeed, the intention was a good one, particularly in view of the imminent arrival of digital technologies in broadcasting. Moreover, it is high time Colombo invested in a multi-purpose tower facility which had the potential to consolidate all broadcasting and telecommunication services, currently scattered, on numerous towers, around the city.

Features of LOTUS TOWER

  • The Revolving Restaurant

    The restaurant operates on roller-based technology which enables diners to dine on a moving deck offering a 360° view of the city of Colombo.

  • The Observation Deck

    The 29th floor is the observation deck and also the last floor which the general public can access. Where you can sight the beauty of the Colombo from the birds-eye view.

  • Inside the Lotus Tower

    The tower has four entrances. Two entrances are using VIPs (distinguished guests and state leaders). A telecommunications museum and restaurant are located on the ground floor. The tower podium consists of 6 floors. The first floor of the podium will accommodate a museum and two exhibition halls. The second floor will use for several conference halls with seating space in excess of 500 people. Restaurants, supermarkets, and food courts will be on the third floor. A 1000-seat auditorium will be on the fourth floor and it will use as a ballroom. The fifth floor will include luxury hotel rooms, and large ballrooms, and the seventh floor will host an observation gallery. The landscaping is planned in the form of a large water park.

  • Lotus Tower’s Initial Purpose

    The lotus-shaped tower is used for communication, observation and other leisure facilities.

  • The shape of the Tower

    The tower house represents the shape of a blossoming lotus. The lotus is a symbol of purity and is said to symbolize the flourishing development of the country. The tower base is designed according to the lotus throne being a symbolic part in Buddhism and the life of the Lord Buddha.

  • Height of the Lotus Tower

    Standing at a height of 350 meters(1,150 ft)with 13 floors, this tower is the tallest self-supported structure in South Asia, the 11th tallest tower in Asia and the 19th tallest tower in the world.

What can we experience at the Lotus Tower in Sri Lanka?

On the ground floor of the Lotus Tower, visitors may encounter bars, renowned restaurants, and gift shops. while the lotus bud shaped structure of the tower is home to a reception hall with a capacity of roughly 400 people and a rotating restaurant that will undoubtedly provide the general public with a unique experience. Additionally, the public will have the extraordinary chance to observe Colombo’s skyline from the tallest observation deck.
